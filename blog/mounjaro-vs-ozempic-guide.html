Mounjaro vs Ozempic: A Doctor's Guide to Choosing the Right Medication

By Dr Abad · 8 June 2026 · 6 min read
A

Dr Abad

GMC-registered doctor and Lead Aesthetic Doctor at Altri Medical Ltd, Altrincham

GLP-1 receptor agonists have transformed the landscape of weight management, offering patients a medically supervised pathway to meaningful, sustained weight loss. The two most discussed medications in this class are semaglutide (sold as Ozempic, Wegovy, and Rybelsus) and tirzepatide (sold as Mounjaro and Zepbound). As a prescribing doctor, I want to give you a clear, evidence-based comparison to help you understand your options.

Important: This article is for informational purposes and does not replace a personalised medical consultation. Weight loss medications should only be prescribed and monitored by a qualified medical professional. Always seek individual medical advice.

How They Work: The Science

Both medications belong to a class called incretin mimetics — they mimic hormones that your body naturally produces after eating to regulate appetite and blood sugar.

Semaglutide (Ozempic / Wegovy)

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist. It mimics the GLP-1 hormone, which signals your brain that you are full, slows gastric emptying, and helps regulate insulin production. The result is reduced appetite, smaller portion sizes, and decreased food cravings — particularly for sugary and high-fat foods.

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro / Zepbound)

Tirzepatide is a dual GLP-1 and GIP receptor agonist — it is the first medication in its class to target two hormone receptors simultaneously. In addition to the appetite-suppressing effects of GLP-1, it also activates GIP receptors, which are involved in fat metabolism and insulin sensitivity. This dual action is the reason tirzepatide has shown greater weight loss in clinical trials.

Efficacy: What the Data Shows

Clinical trial data from the major studies gives us a clear picture of what to expect:

Measure Semaglutide 2.4mg Tirzepatide 15mg
Average weight loss at 68–72 weeks ~15–17% of body weight ~20–22% of body weight
Patients achieving ≥10% weight loss ~69% ~83%
Patients achieving ≥20% weight loss ~32% ~57%
Key trial STEP programme SURMOUNT programme

These are impressive figures, but I always counsel patients to interpret clinical trial data carefully. Trial participants receive significant dietary and exercise support alongside the medication, which contributes to the results. In real-world practice, individual responses vary considerably.

Side Effects

Both medications share a similar side effect profile because they work through related pathways. The most common side effects are gastrointestinal and tend to be most pronounced during dose escalation:

  • Nausea — the most common side effect for both; usually improves after the first few weeks
  • Diarrhoea or constipation
  • Vomiting — less common but can occur, particularly if meals are too large
  • Fatigue and dizziness — particularly in the initial weeks
  • Injection site reactions — mild redness or itching at the injection site

Rarer but more serious side effects include pancreatitis, gallbladder disease, and — in extremely rare cases — medullary thyroid cancer (the medications carry a boxed warning for patients with a personal or family history of MTC or MEN2).

In my clinical experience, tirzepatide may cause slightly more nausea during the initial titration phase, though this generally settles. Both medications are well tolerated once patients reach their maintenance dose.

Administration and Dosing

Both medications are administered as a once-weekly subcutaneous injection, which patients learn to do themselves at home using a pre-filled pen. The process is straightforward and the needles are very fine.

  • Semaglutide: Starts at 0.25mg weekly, escalated over 16–20 weeks to a maintenance dose of 0.5mg, 1mg, 1.7mg, or 2.4mg
  • Tirzepatide: Starts at 2.5mg weekly, escalated over 20+ weeks to a maintenance dose of 5mg, 10mg, or 15mg

Gradual dose escalation is critical to minimise side effects. I never rush the titration — patient comfort and tolerability come first.

Who Is Each Medication Suited To?

The decision between semaglutide and tirzepatide is not simply "the one that causes more weight loss is better." Several factors influence the choice:

  • Weight loss goals: If you have a significant amount of weight to lose, tirzepatide's greater efficacy may be advantageous
  • Diabetes status: Both can be used in patients with type 2 diabetes, though your prescribing doctor will consider your individual glycaemic targets
  • Tolerability: Some patients tolerate one medication better than the other — this can only be determined through experience
  • Availability and cost: Both are available through private prescription in the UK, but prices and availability fluctuate
  • Contraindications: Personal and family medical history may make one medication more appropriate than the other

The Importance of Medical Supervision

These are powerful medications that affect multiple body systems. Obtaining them without medical supervision — from online sources, unregulated suppliers, or through "diet clinics" without proper medical oversight — carries real risks. A qualified doctor will screen you appropriately, monitor your progress, manage side effects, and ensure the medication is safe for you as an individual.

At our clinic, every patient undergoes a thorough medical assessment before any weight loss medication is prescribed. This includes a detailed medical history, blood tests, body composition analysis, and a discussion about realistic expectations. We provide ongoing monitoring with regular follow-ups to ensure safety and optimise results.

Cost Considerations

Both medications are only available privately in the UK for weight management (NHS prescribing criteria are restrictive). Prices typically range from £150–£300 per month depending on the medication and dose. While this represents a significant investment, many patients find the health benefits — improved blood markers, reduced joint pain, better sleep, and increased energy — justify the cost.

A Final Thought

GLP-1 medications are a powerful tool, but they are not a magic solution. The best outcomes come when medication is combined with dietary changes, physical activity, and — importantly — the psychological support to sustain new habits long-term. As a doctor, my role is not just to prescribe, but to support you through a comprehensive weight management journey.
