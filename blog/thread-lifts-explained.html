Thread Lifts: The Non-Surgical Facelift Explained

By Dr Abad · 2 June 2026 · 4 min read
A

Dr Abad

GMC-registered doctor and Lead Aesthetic Doctor at Altri Medical Ltd, Altrincham

Thread lifts have rapidly grown in popularity over recent years, and for good reason. They offer a way to lift and reposition sagging facial tissue without the incisions, general anaesthesia, and extended recovery associated with a surgical facelift. But thread lifts are not a substitute for surgery — they occupy a specific, valuable place in the spectrum of facial rejuvenation.

How Thread Lifts Work

The concept is elegantly simple. Fine, absorbable threads are inserted beneath the skin using a needle or cannula. Once positioned, the threads are gently pulled to lift the underlying tissue, and the barbs (or cones) along the thread anchor it in place. This creates an immediate lifting effect while also triggering the body's natural collagen production as it gradually absorbs the thread material.

The dual action is what makes thread lifts unique: you get both an immediate mechanical lift and a longer-term regenerative effect from collagen stimulation. In my practice, this combination produces results that look natural and continue to improve in the weeks following treatment.

Types of Threads

Not all threads are the same. The three main categories used in aesthetic practice are:

PDO (Polydioxanone) Threads

PDO threads have been used in surgery for decades — most commonly as absorbable sutures. In aesthetic medicine, they are the most widely used thread type. PDO threads are fully absorbed by the body over 6–9 months and stimulate moderate collagen production. They are well-suited for patients with mild to moderate sagging who want a subtle lift and skin quality improvement.

PLLA (Poly-L-Lactic Acid) Threads

PLLA threads, made from the same material as the collagen-stimulating Sculptra, last longer than PDO — typically 12–18 months. They provide a stronger collagen-stimulating effect, making them ideal for patients who want both lifting and significant skin thickening. PLLA threads are particularly effective for patients with thinner skin.

PCL (Polycaprolactone) Threads

PCL threads are the longest-lasting option, maintaining their structure for up to 24 months. They provide the strongest lifting effect and the most sustained collagen stimulation. PCL threads are generally reserved for patients with more pronounced sagging who want a longer-lasting result without surgery.

Which Areas Can Be Treated?

Thread lifts are versatile and can address sagging in several areas of the face and neck:

  • Mid-face and cheeks: lifting the cheek pad to restore volume and reduce nasolabial folds
  • Jawline: defining and tightening the jawline, reducing jowling
  • Lower face: lifting the corners of the mouth and addressing marionette lines
  • Neck: tightening loose neck skin and improving the cervicomental angle
  • Brow: a subtle brow lift to open up the eye area
  • Decolletage: tightening skin on the chest and upper neck area

The Procedure: What to Expect

A thread lift is typically performed under local anaesthetic and takes 45–90 minutes depending on the number of threads and areas treated. The process involves:

  1. Consultation and planning: I assess your facial anatomy, discuss your goals, and mark the precise insertion and exit points
  2. Anaesthesia: A local anaesthetic is applied to ensure comfort throughout the procedure
  3. Thread insertion: Threads are carefully placed under the skin using a fine needle or cannula
  4. Lifting and securing: Each thread is gently tensioned to lift the tissue, then anchored in place
  5. Trimming: Excess thread is trimmed flush with the skin, and the entry points are closed

Recovery Timeline

  • Days 1–3: Mild swelling, bruising, and tenderness are normal. Most patients return to work within 2–3 days
  • Week 1: Swelling and bruising subside significantly. Visible lifting is apparent
  • Weeks 2–4: Initial settling as the threads anchor fully. Results continue to refine
  • Months 2–6: Collagen production around the threads gradually improves skin quality, with the full effect visible at around 3–6 months

How Long Do Results Last?

The longevity of results depends primarily on the type of thread used:

  • PDO: 12–18 months
  • PLLA: 18–24 months
  • PCL: up to 24–36 months
A thread lift sits in the "Goldilocks zone" between non-invasive treatments (like fillers and polynucleotides) and surgery. It is not meant to replicate a surgical facelift — it is designed for patients with early to moderate sagging who want meaningful lifting without the commitment and recovery of surgery.

Is a Thread Lift Right for You?

The ideal candidate for a thread lift is someone with mild to moderate facial sagging who wants visible lifting with minimal downtime, and who is not yet ready — or does not want — a surgical procedure. Thread lifts are also excellent for patients seeking to delay the need for surgery by maintaining a lifted, youthful appearance.

During a consultation, I assess your facial anatomy, discuss your goals, and recommend the most appropriate thread type and number. Not every patient is a candidate for thread lifting — and as a doctor, I will always be honest about whether it is the right option for your specific situation.
