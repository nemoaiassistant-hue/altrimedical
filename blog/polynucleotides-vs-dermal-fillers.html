Polynucleotides vs Dermal Fillers: Which Is Right for You?

By Dr Abad · 15 June 2026 · 5 min read
A

Dr Abad

GMC-registered doctor and Lead Aesthetic Doctor at Altri Medical Ltd, Altrincham

Two of the most frequently discussed treatments in my clinic are polynucleotides and dermal fillers. Both can deliver remarkable improvements to the skin, but they work in fundamentally different ways. Understanding the distinction is essential to making an informed decision about which treatment — or combination — is right for your concerns.

What Are Polynucleotides?

Polynucleotides are highly purified DNA fragments derived from natural sources, typically trout or salmon roe. When injected into the skin, they stimulate your own cells to regenerate tissue, produce more collagen, and improve microcirculation. Rather than adding a foreign substance, they activate your skin's intrinsic repair mechanisms.

The result is gradual improvement in skin quality — enhanced hydration, firmer texture, reduced fine lines, and a more even tone. I often describe polynucleotides as "feeding the soil" rather than "building on top of it."

What Are Dermal Fillers?

Dermal fillers, most commonly based on hyaluronic acid (HA), are a gel-like substance injected beneath the skin to restore lost volume, smooth deeper lines, and enhance facial contours. HA is a naturally occurring molecule in the body that attracts and holds water, which is why it produces immediate plumping effects.

Fillers are a direct, structural intervention: they replace what has been lost. If the cheeks have flattened, the lips have thinned, or deep nasolabial folds have formed, fillers can provide instant, visible correction.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Feature Polynucleotides Dermal Fillers
Primary action Cellular regeneration & collagen stimulation Volume replacement & structural support
Onset of results Gradual over 2–4 weeks Immediate
Duration 6–12 months (improves with courses) 6–18 months (varies by product)
Best for Skin quality, texture, fine lines Volume loss, deep folds, lip augmentation
Downtime Minimal (mild swelling 24–48 hrs) Minimal (possible bruising)
Treatment course 3 sessions, 2–4 weeks apart 1–2 sessions per area

Who Is Each Treatment Suited To?

Ideal candidates for polynucleotides

  • People concerned with overall skin quality — dullness, uneven texture, or early signs of ageing
  • Those seeking subtle, natural-looking improvement rather than dramatic change
  • Patients who want a regenerative approach with long-term skin health benefits
  • Individuals who may not yet need volume replacement but want to invest in prevention

Ideal candidates for dermal fillers

  • People with noticeable volume loss in the cheeks, temples, or lips
  • Those with deep nasolabial folds, marionette lines, or jawline sagging
  • Patients wanting immediate, visible results
  • Individuals seeking contouring and definition in specific areas

Combining Both Treatments

In my practice, I increasingly recommend combining polynucleotides and fillers for comprehensive facial rejuvenation. The polynucleotides improve the quality and health of the skin from within, while fillers restore structural volume where needed. Together, they address both the "canvas" (skin quality) and the "architecture" (facial structure) for a more complete, natural result.

The best outcomes in aesthetic medicine come from treating the whole face holistically — not chasing individual lines or features in isolation. Polynucleotides and fillers used together exemplify this approach.

Cost Considerations

Polynucleotides typically require an initial course of three sessions, so the upfront investment is higher, but the cumulative improvement in skin quality can reduce the need for other treatments over time. Dermal fillers are priced per syringe, making the cost more predictable for single areas, though larger treatment plans can add up. During your consultation, we will discuss a realistic treatment plan and transparent pricing tailored to your goals.

Making Your Decision

The right choice depends on your primary concerns, your budget, and your timeline for results. There is no universal "better" treatment — only the treatment that is better suited to you. I always recommend starting with a thorough consultation where we assess your skin, discuss your goals, and build a personalised plan.
