Botox Myths Debunked: What Really Happens During Treatment

By Dr Abad · 10 June 2026 · 4 min read
A

Dr Abad

GMC-registered doctor and Lead Aesthetic Doctor at Altri Medical Ltd, Altrincham

In my years of practice, I have found that anti-wrinkle injections — commonly known by the brand name Botox — are perhaps the most misunderstood treatment in aesthetic medicine. Misinformation spreads quickly, and patients often arrive at their first consultation with preconceptions that simply are not true. Let me address the most common myths and explain what actually happens during treatment.

Myth 1: "Botox will give me a frozen face"

This is by far the most common concern I hear. The reality is that a "frozen" result is not the goal of modern aesthetic practice — and it is entirely preventable with correct dosing and technique. I use careful, targeted placement to soften lines while preserving your natural expressions. The aim is for people to notice you look refreshed and well-rested, not "done."

Myth 2: "Botox is painful"

Fact: The needles used for anti-wrinkle injections are extremely fine — much thinner than those used for blood tests. Most patients describe the sensation as a mild pinch or tiny sting that lasts seconds. The entire treatment for common areas (forehead, frown lines, crow's feet) takes around 10–15 minutes. We can also apply a topical numbing cream beforehand if you are particularly sensitive.

Myth 3: "Botox is addictive"

Fact: There is no physiological addiction to botulinum toxin. It does not contain any addictive substances. What some people perceive as "addiction" is simply that they prefer how they look with the treatment and choose to maintain those results. That is a personal choice, not a chemical dependency.

Myth 4: "Botox is only for older people"

While many patients start treatment in their thirties or forties, there is a growing trend of younger patients — often in their mid-twenties — choosing preventative anti-wrinkle treatment. The logic is sound: by reducing repetitive muscle movement that causes dynamic lines, you can delay the formation of static wrinkles that become etched into the skin over time. That said, there is no "right age" — the right time is whenever the treatment aligns with your personal goals.

Myth 5: "Botox is unsafe"

Fact: Botulinum toxin type A has been used in medicine for over 40 years and in aesthetics for over 20 years. It is one of the most studied pharmaceutical products in the world. When administered by a qualified, GMC-registered medical professional in appropriate doses, it has an excellent safety profile. The risks are minimal and well understood.

Myth 6: "Botox will migrate and affect other areas"

Fact: While migration was a concern with older injection techniques, modern practitioners are trained to use specific dosing, precise placement, and post-treatment precautions (such as not rubbing the area or lying flat for several hours) to minimise any risk of spread. In experienced hands, migration is extremely rare.

Myth 7: "Once you start, you can never stop"

Fact: You can stop at any time. If you discontinue treatment, the treated muscles will gradually regain full movement over 3–6 months, and your appearance will simply return to its natural state. The lines that were there before treatment may gradually reappear, but they will not be worse than they were originally.

Myth 8: "All Botox is the same"

Fact: "Botox" is a brand name (manufactured by Allergan). There are other licensed botulinum toxin products available in the UK, including Bocouture (Xeomin), Azzalure (Dysport), and Nuceiva (Jeuveau). They have slightly different formulations and diffusion characteristics. A qualified doctor will recommend the most appropriate product for your specific needs.

What Actually Happens During Treatment

Understanding the procedure can go a long way in putting your mind at ease. Here is what to expect when you visit our Altrincham clinic:

  1. Consultation: We discuss your concerns, medical history, and treatment goals. I will assess your facial anatomy and explain exactly what I recommend and why.
  2. Preparation: The skin is cleansed. A topical numbing cream can be applied if desired.
  3. Treatment: Using a very fine needle, I make a series of small injections into the target muscles. The number of injections varies by treatment area — typically around 5 for frown lines, 3–5 for the forehead, and 2–3 per side for crow's feet.
  4. Aftercare: I provide clear post-treatment instructions. You should avoid rubbing the area, strenuous exercise, and lying flat for 4–6 hours. Results begin to appear within 3–5 days and reach full effect at 10–14 days.
The difference between a good result and a great one comes down to the skill and experience of the practitioner. Always choose a GMC-registered doctor who understands facial anatomy and takes the time to personalise your treatment.

The Bottom Line

Anti-wrinkle injections are a safe, well-established, and highly effective treatment when administered by a qualified medical professional. The myths persist largely because of outdated information and high-profile cases of poor practice. In a clinical setting with an experienced doctor, the treatment is precise, subtle, and entirely manageable.

If you have been considering anti-wrinkle treatment but have been put off by things you have heard, I would encourage you to book a consultation. We can discuss your concerns in a relaxed, private environment, and I will give you honest, medically grounded advice.
