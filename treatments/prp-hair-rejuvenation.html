Overview

PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) hair rejuvenation is a regenerative treatment that harnesses your body's natural growth factors to stimulate hair follicles. A small sample of your blood is processed to concentrate the platelets, which are then injected into the scalp. These platelets release growth factors that improve blood supply, increase hair follicle size, and trigger the growth phase of the hair cycle.

Benefits

  • Uses your own blood — no synthetic chemicals
  • Stimulates dormant hair follicles to re-enter the active growth phase
  • Improves hair thickness, density, and scalp health
  • Safe, natural treatment with minimal risk of allergic reaction
  • Can be combined with other hair loss treatments
  • Effective for androgenetic alopecia and early-stage hair loss

The Procedure

  1. Consultation: Your doctor examines your scalp and determines if PRP is suitable for your type of hair loss.
  2. Blood draw: A small amount of blood (10-20ml) is drawn from your arm.
  3. Processing: Blood is centrifuged to separate the platelet-rich plasma — approximately 10 minutes.
  4. Injection: Concentrated PRP is injected into the scalp at hair follicle level using a fine needle.
  5. Aftercare: Post-treatment instructions provided. Mild tenderness and redness are normal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many sessions do I need?

An initial course of 3-4 sessions spaced 4-6 weeks apart, followed by maintenance every 3-6 months.

Is PRP effective for hair loss?

Clinical studies show PRP is effective for many patients, particularly those with androgenetic alopecia and diffuse thinning in early stages.

Does it hurt?

A topical numbing cream is applied. Most patients describe the sensation as mild pressure or tingling.

How long until I see results?

Reduced shedding within 2-3 months. Visible new growth at 3-6 months, optimal results over 6-12 months.
